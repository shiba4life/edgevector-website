BYLAWS OF EDGE VECTOR FOUNDATION

---

## Article I — Name and Purpose

**1.1 Name**

The name of the organization is Edge Vector Foundation ("Foundation").

**1.2 Principal Office**

The principal office of the Foundation shall be at 2328 Delmar Dr E, Seattle, WA 98102.

**1.3 Purpose**

The Foundation is organized exclusively for charitable and scientific purposes, including but not limited to:

- advancing consumer data ownership, privacy, and portability
- funding and conducting research into secure, user-controlled data systems
- developing and maintaining open-source software that enables individuals to own, control, and share their data without surrendering custody to third parties
- owning and stewarding intellectual property for public benefit

The Foundation may form and own subsidiaries to further its mission.

---

## Article II — No Members

The Foundation shall have no members.

All corporate powers shall be exercised by or under the authority of the Board of Directors.

---

## Article III — Board of Directors

**3.1 General Powers**

The Board of Directors ("Board") shall have full authority over the Foundation, including:

- governance and strategic direction
- ownership and control of subsidiaries
- approval of major transactions
- stewardship of intellectual property

**3.2 Founder**

Tom Tang is the Founder of the Foundation ("Founder"). The Founder's rights and powers under these bylaws are personal to Tom Tang and are not transferable or assignable.

In the event the Founder resigns, becomes permanently incapacitated, or dies, all reserved powers of the Founder under these bylaws shall terminate. Thereafter, the Board shall govern by majority vote of the directors then in office, and quorum shall require a majority of directors then in office. The Board may, by unanimous vote, designate a successor Chair.

For purposes of these bylaws, "permanently incapacitated" means the Founder is unable to perform the duties of a director for a continuous period of one hundred eighty (180) days, as determined by the written opinions of two (2) licensed physicians who are not related to any director or officer of the Foundation.

**3.3 Number and Composition**

The Board shall consist of no fewer than three (3) and no more than nine (9) directors. The initial directors shall be:

- Tom Tang (Founder)
- Fei Jia
- Cedric Hurst

Thereafter, directors shall be appointed or replaced in accordance with Sections 3.6 and 3.7.

**3.4 Term**

- The Founder shall serve as a director for as long as he wishes to remain on the Board, with no term limit
- All other directors serve a one (1) year term and may be reappointed

**3.5 Chair**

- The Founder shall serve as Chair of the Board for as long as he serves as a director
- The Chair presides over meetings and maintains governance continuity
- If the Chair is absent, the President shall preside

**3.6 Removal**

A director may be removed only by:

- majority vote of the Board **AND**
- approval of the Founder

**3.7 Vacancies**

Vacancies may be filled by:

- majority vote of the Board **AND**
- approval of the Founder

---

## Article IV — Officers

**4.1 Officers**

The officers of the Foundation shall be a President, Secretary, and Treasurer. One person may hold multiple offices, except that the President and Secretary shall not be the same person.

**4.2 Appointment**

Officers shall be appointed by the Board. The initial officers shall be:

| Officer | Name |
|---------|------|
| President | Tom Tang |
| Secretary | Cedric Hurst |
| Treasurer | Fei Jia |

Thereafter, officers shall be appointed by majority vote of the Board, subject to the Founder's approval.

**4.3 President**

The President shall be the chief executive officer, execute documents on behalf of the Foundation, and manage day-to-day operations.

**4.4 Secretary**

The Secretary shall keep minutes of Board meetings, maintain corporate records, and provide notices as required.

**4.5 Treasurer**

The Treasurer shall have custody of corporate funds, maintain financial records, and provide financial reports to the Board.

**4.6 Term and Removal**

Officers serve one-year terms and may be reappointed. Officers may be removed by majority vote of the Board with the Founder's approval. The Founder's service as President shall have no term limit and shall continue for as long as the Founder wishes.

---

## Article V — Meetings

**5.1 Regular Meetings**

The Board shall meet as needed.

**5.2 Special Meetings**

May be called by any director with at least ten (10) days written notice to all directors.

**5.3 Quorum**

A quorum requires at least two (2) directors, **including the Founder**.

No meeting is valid without the Founder present.

**5.4 Voting**

Standard matters require a majority vote of directors present at a duly constituted meeting.

**5.5 Action Without Meeting**

Any action may be taken without a meeting if all directors consent in writing (including email).

---

## Article VI — Reserved Matters (Founder Approval Required)

The following actions require:

**(i) Majority Board Approval AND (ii) the Founder's explicit consent**

Reserved Matters include:

- Creation, sale, or dissolution of subsidiaries
- Transfer, licensing, or disposition of Foundation intellectual property
- Any change in ownership structure of subsidiaries
- Appointment or removal of officers or subsidiary managers
- Approval of profit participation (PI) pool above designated threshold
- Mergers, acquisitions, or sale of assets
- Amendments to these bylaws
- Addition or removal of directors

---

## Article VII — Subsidiaries

**7.1 Authority**

The Foundation may form or acquire subsidiaries to carry out its mission.

**7.2 Ownership**

The Foundation shall own 100% of the equity of any subsidiary unless otherwise approved under Reserved Matters.

**7.3 Management**

The Board shall appoint management of subsidiaries. Removal of subsidiary managers requires compliance with Reserved Matters.

---

## Article VIII — Conflict of Interest

**8.1 Disclosure**

Any director or officer with a financial interest in a transaction must:

- disclose the nature of the interest prior to any Board action on the transaction
- recuse themselves from voting on the transaction

**8.2 Annual Disclosure**

Each director and officer shall annually sign a written statement that:

- identifies any entity in which the director or officer has a financial interest or serves as a director, officer, trustee, partner, or employee
- identifies any transaction in which the Foundation is a party and in which the director or officer has a direct or indirect financial interest
- affirms that the director or officer has received, read, and understood the Foundation's conflict of interest policy and agrees to comply with it

Annual statements shall be collected by the Secretary within thirty (30) days of the beginning of each fiscal year and upon appointment of any new director or officer. The Secretary shall maintain all statements in the Foundation's corporate records.

**8.3 Founder Reserved Rights**

The Founder's consent rights under Article VI (Reserved Matters) are a separate governance right, not a Board vote. The Founder may exercise Reserved Matters consent on transactions in which the Founder has a financial interest, provided the interest has been disclosed under Sections 8.1 and 8.2. The Founder shall still recuse from the Board vote on such transactions.

**8.4 Approval**

Transactions involving conflicts must be:

- fair and reasonable
- in the best interest of the Foundation
- approved by the remaining non-conflicted directors (at least one non-conflicted director must approve)

---

## Article IX — Compensation

Directors shall not receive compensation for board service unless approved under Reserved Matters.

The Foundation may pay reasonable compensation for services rendered in other capacities.

---

## Article X — Indemnification

The Foundation shall indemnify directors and officers to the fullest extent permitted by law.

---

## Article XI — Fiscal Year

The fiscal year of the Foundation shall end on December 31.

---

## Article XII — Dissolution

Upon dissolution of the Foundation, the Board shall, after paying or making provision for the payment of all liabilities, distribute all remaining assets to one or more organizations organized and operated exclusively for charitable or scientific purposes and exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, in accordance with RCW 24.03A. No part of the assets shall be distributed to any director or officer of the Foundation.

---

## Article XIII — Amendments

These bylaws may be amended only by:

**(i) Majority Board Approval AND (ii) the Founder's explicit consent**
